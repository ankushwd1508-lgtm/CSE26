DRAGON BALL LEGACY

Akira Toriyama (1955–2024) was a legendary Japanese manga artist best known for creating Dragon Ball, one of the most influential anime and manga franchises in history. He began his career with the hit comedy series Dr. Slump, which established his playful art style and storytelling skills. In 1984, Toriyama created Dragon Ball, originally inspired by the Chinese classic Journey to the West, with Son Goku based on the Monkey King. The early story focused on adventure, humor, and martial arts, but as the manga grew in popularity, Toriyama gradually shifted it toward intense battles, power progression, and science-fiction elements. This evolution led to the anime adaptation by Toei Animation in 1986, and later Dragon Ball Z in 1989, which defined the modern shōnen battle anime with concepts like Super Saiyan transformations, long training arcs, and epic villain fights. The series became a global phenomenon, influencing countless creators and shaping future anime such as Naruto, One Piece, and Bleach. Over the decades, the franchise expanded into multiple series, movies, games, and merchandise, making characters like Goku and Vegeta global pop-culture icons. Toriyama’s legacy lives on through Dragon Ball, which remains a cornerstone of anime culture and continues to inspire new generations around the world.
